Here are descriptions of some projects we have worked on while studying and working at the School of Computer Science at Reykjavik University. The projects were done under the supervision of Hannes Högni Vilhjámsson who leads the Socially Expressive Computing Group at the CADIA research center.

Video showing some projects

These are some clips showing a few projects that we have worked on.

Character Territoriality: Spatial awareness and navigation (2018)

This project is about creating territorial behavior for virtual people. Ari was working on this project a large part of the year 2018 at the CADIA research center. This picture shows a scene with some virtual characters. Special maps are generated based on the environment and they are used to create navigation paths for the characters. This project is part of a larger research project lead by Claudio Pedica.

Djúpivogur 3D (2018)

Research project at CADIA (Reykjavik University) that Hörður has been working on. This is a city planning project for a town in Iceland where virtual reality is used to convey proposed changes in the city planning. A part of the town of Djúpivogur as been created and the proposed changes have been added.

SoCueVR - A Virtual Reality Game for Social Training (spring 2018)

This picture and the video below it show an application that Ari created as part of his masters project. The application was a virtual reality game for teaching social skills. The application is described in detail in his thesis. The game has virtual characters whose behavior is based uppon research in the social sciences on non verbal communication.

Procedural City (2018)

Research project at CADIA (Reykjavik University) that Hörður worked on. In this project large and very different city environments can be created with ease (the click of a button). These environments are then used to run psychological studies within virtual reality.

Vogabyggð 3D (2017)

This is a research project that we worked on at the CADIA research center. In this project a replica was made of a particular place in Iceland to be used in a research study within virtual reality. Participants would teleport around the virtual environment and were presented with likert scale questions in the virtual reality environment.

Maze VR (2017)

This was a small game made for virtual reality by Hörður. In this game people can freely walk around the corridors of an endless maze. The virtual environment matches a physical walking space of 3mx3m, and therefore allowing people to physically walk around the virtual environment, where as the usual mode of locomotion is teleportation. This allows for a much greater immersion and presence within the virtual environment. When people walk around, the maze environment is constantly changing giving the illusion that you are going further and further into the maze. While somebody is navigating the virtual maze it is possible for another person to sit at a computer monitor and control certain aspects of the maze experience, i.e. the lighting and sounds can be affected as well as doors, props and characters can be controlled.

Inducing Phobia Response Using Virtual Reality (summer 2016)

These screenshot show virtual environments created for a project during the summer of 2016 at the CADIA research center. The purpose of this project was to study the feasibility of using virtual reality to induce phobia responses. The study was carried out by the company deCODE genetics. Five virtual environments were created for five different phobias which were fear of heights, fear of spiders, fear of water, fear of sharp objects and claustrophobia. The HTC Vive virtual reality headset was used in the study. The project was nominated for the Innovation Award of the President of Iceland 2017. A final report describes the project in some detail.
