Here are descriptions of some projects we have worked on while studying and working at the School of Computer Science at Reykjavik University.

Character Territoriality: Spatial awareness and navigation (2018)

This project is about creating territorial behavior for virtual people. Ari was working on this project a large part of the year 2018 at the CADIA research center. This picture shows a scene with some virtual characters. Special maps are generated based on the environment and they are used to create navigation paths for the characters. This project is part of a larger research project lead by Claudio Pedica.

SoCueVR - A Virtual Reality Game for Social Training (spring 2018)

This picture and the video below it show an application that Ari created as part of his masters project. His superviser was Hannes Högni Vilhjámsson The application was a virtual reality game for teaching social skills. The application is described in detail in his thesis. The game has virtual characters whose behavior is based uppon research in the social sciences on non verbal communication.

Inducing Phobia Response Using Virtual Reality (sumer 2016)

This screenshot shows a virtual environment used for inducing the fear of water. This is part of a project we worked on during the summer of 2016 at the CADIA research center. Our superviser during the project was Hannes Högni Vilhjámsson who leads the Socially Expressive Computing Group. The purpose of this project was to study the feasibility of using virtual reality to induce phobia responses. The study was carried out by the company deCODE genetics. Five virtual environments were created for five different phobias which were fear of heights, fear of spiders, fear of water, fear of sharp objects and claustrophobia. The HTC Vive virtual reality headset was used in the study. The project was nominated for the Innovation Award of the President of Iceland 2017. A final report describes the project in some detail.
